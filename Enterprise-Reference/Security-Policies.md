\# Security Policies



\---



\# Apex Manufacturing, Inc.



\## Purpose



This document defines the information security policies established by Apex Manufacturing, Inc. These policies are intended to protect company systems, data, employees, and customers by promoting secure technology practices across the organization.



These policies apply to all employees, contractors, interns, vendors, and third-party users with access to company resources.



\---



\# Security Objectives



The Information Security Program is designed to:



\- Protect confidential business information

\- Maintain system availability

\- Preserve data integrity

\- Reduce organizational risk

\- Meet regulatory and contractual obligations

\- Support business continuity

\- Promote cybersecurity awareness



\---



\# Acceptable Use Policy



Company-owned devices and technology resources are provided for authorized business purposes.



Employees must:



\- Use company equipment responsibly.

\- Follow all IT and security policies.

\- Report suspected security incidents immediately.

\- Lock workstations when unattended.

\- Protect company information from unauthorized disclosure.



Employees must not:



\- Install unauthorized software.

\- Disable security controls.

\- Share passwords.

\- Use company devices for illegal activities.

\- Connect unauthorized hardware to the corporate network.



\---



\# Password Policy



Passwords must meet the following requirements:



| Requirement | Standard |

|------------|----------|

| Minimum Length | 14 characters |

| Complexity | Uppercase, lowercase, number, special character |

| Password Reuse | Last 24 passwords remembered |

| Maximum Age | 90 days |

| Minimum Age | 1 day |

| Account Lockout | 5 failed attempts |

| Lockout Duration | 15 minutes |



Passwords must never be written down or shared with another individual.



\---



\# Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)



Multi-Factor Authentication is required for:



\- Microsoft 365

\- VPN access

\- Administrative accounts

\- Remote management tools

\- Cloud services



Approved authentication methods include:



\- Microsoft Authenticator

\- Hardware security key

\- Time-based one-time password (TOTP)



SMS authentication should only be used when approved by IT.



\---



\# Least Privilege



Users are granted only the permissions required to perform their job responsibilities.



Administrative privileges require documented approval and must be reviewed regularly.



Temporary administrative access should expire immediately after work is completed.



\---



\# Account Management



The IT department is responsible for:



\- User account creation

\- Account modification

\- Account disablement

\- Account deletion

\- Group membership management



Accounts for terminated employees must be disabled immediately upon notification from Human Resources.



\---



\# Endpoint Security



All company endpoints must:



\- Run Microsoft Defender Antivirus

\- Receive automatic Windows Updates

\- Have BitLocker enabled where supported

\- Use Windows Firewall

\- Be joined to the corporate domain

\- Receive centralized policy updates



Employees may not disable endpoint protection software.



\---



\# Remote Access



Remote access requires:



\- Company-approved VPN

\- Multi-Factor Authentication

\- Company-managed device

\- Active Directory credentials



Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) access is restricted to authorized administrators.



\---



\# Email Security



Employees should:



\- Verify unknown senders.

\- Report phishing emails.

\- Avoid opening unexpected attachments.

\- Never provide passwords by email.

\- Verify requests involving financial transactions.



Suspicious emails should be reported to the Information Security team immediately.



\---



\# Data Classification



| Classification | Description |

|---------------|-------------|

| Public | Approved for public release |

| Internal | Intended for employees only |

| Confidential | Sensitive business information |

| Restricted | Highly sensitive information requiring additional protection |



Employees must handle data according to its assigned classification.



\---



\# Removable Media



The use of USB storage devices is restricted.



Only company-approved encrypted storage devices may be used when business needs require removable media.



Unknown USB devices must never be connected to company systems.



\---



\# Patch Management



Microsoft Windows updates are deployed according to the organization's maintenance schedule.



Critical security updates should be installed as soon as practical after testing.



Emergency patches may be deployed outside normal maintenance windows when necessary.



\---



\# Incident Reporting



Employees must immediately report:



\- Lost or stolen devices

\- Phishing attempts

\- Malware infections

\- Unauthorized access

\- Suspicious system behavior

\- Data loss

\- Policy violations



Reports should be submitted through the Help Desk or Security Operations.



\---



\# Physical Security



Employees are responsible for protecting physical company assets.



Requirements include:



\- Wearing identification badges

\- Preventing tailgating

\- Securing laptops

\- Locking offices when unattended

\- Returning company property upon separation



\---



\# Security Awareness



Employees are required to complete annual cybersecurity awareness training.



Topics include:



\- Phishing

\- Social engineering

\- Password security

\- Data handling

\- Safe internet practices

\- Physical security

\- Incident reporting



\---



\# Policy Violations



Violations of security policies may result in:



\- Additional training

\- Removal of system access

\- Disciplinary action

\- Termination of employment

\- Legal action where appropriate



\---



\# Policy Review



These security policies are reviewed annually by the Information Technology department and executive leadership.



Revisions may also occur following:



\- Security incidents

\- Regulatory changes

\- Technology changes

\- Risk assessments
